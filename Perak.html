PERAK

Perak

Perak, a Malaysian state, combines historical charm and natural beauty seamlessly. From the captivating markets and ancient temples of Ipoh to the lush landscapes that envelop the region, Perak is a tapestry of cultural heritage. Renowned for traditional arts such as Labu Sayong pottery, it encapsulates the rich history and artistry of the region, making it a captivating destination for those seeking a blend of heritage and scenic wonders.


BIRCH MEMORIAL CLOCK TOWER

Birch Memorial Clock Tower

The Birch Memorial Clock Tower, situated in Ipoh, Perak, is an elegant historical landmark erected in 1909. This iconic structure pays tribute to J.W.W. Birch, the first British Resident of Perak, who was assassinated in 1875. Combining colonial and Moorish architectural elements, the clock tower stands tall as a symbol of Perak's colonial history and honors the memory of a pivotal figure. Its timeless design and historical significance make the Birch Memorial Clock Tower not just a monument but a distinguished part of Ipoh's cityscape and a testament to the region's rich heritage.

Labu Sayong

Labu Sayong, a traditional pottery craft originating from Perak, Malaysia, is a unique and distinctive clay vessel. Crafted in the Kuala Kangsar district, it showcases bulbous shapes with narrow necks, traditionally used for storing water.

The meticulous crafting process involves shaping and molding the clay, resulting in functional yet intricately designed pots. Known not only for its cooling properties but also for intricate patterns, Labu Sayong stands as a symbol of Perak's cultural heritage and the skilled artistry passed down through generations, offering both utility and aesthetic appeal.

Ipoh Hor Fun


Ipoh Hor Fun

A signature dish from Perak, Ipoh Hor Fun is a delicious noodle soup featuring flat rice noodles, shredded chicken, and prawns in a flavorful broth.

Lemang Perak


Lemang Perak

A traditional Malay dish often enjoyed during festive occasions, Lemang Perak consists of glutinous rice and coconut milk cooked in bamboo, resulting in a uniquely fragrant and sticky rice cake.

Heong Peah


Heong Peah

Known as Ipoh's signature biscuit, Heong Peah is a flaky pastry filled with a sweet and aromatic mixture of maltose, shallots, and sesame seeds. It is a popular local snack appreciated for its distinctive taste and texture.
