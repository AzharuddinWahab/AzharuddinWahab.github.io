Gunung Kinabalu

Gunung Kinabalu, which stands majestically in the Malaysian state of Sabah, is Southeast Asia's highest peak and a natural wonder of incomparable beauty. This landmark mountain is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its unique biodiversity and breathtaking scenery.

One of the highlights of the hike is reaching the summit, known as Low's Peak, which offers amazing panoramic views. The sunrise from this vantage point colours the sky in orange and pink, creating a bizarre and breathtaking spectacle.

Zumazau Dance

Zumzau dance is a mesmerising traditional dance that originated in Sabah, Malaysia, with its varied cultural tapestry. This dynamic and rhythmic performance is an important component of the Kadazandusun people's cultural legacy.

The Zumzau dance is frequently performed at festivals, celebrations, and other notable events to highlight the Kadazandusun people's unique cultural identity. Dancers dressed in vivid traditional costumes move smoothly to the rhythm of traditional music, resulting in a visually striking and entertaining display.

Ambuyat

Ambuyat is a delicious meal that has a distinct place in Sabah's culinary history.The sago palm, which grows abundantly in the region, is central to Ambuyat. The dish is made by removing the starchy core of the sago palm and converting it into a sticky, gooey consistency. This unusual texture is what distinguishes Ambuyat, making it an intriguing culinary experience.

Ambuyat is typically served with a variety of savoury side dishes, as well as dipping sauces, gravies, and condiments. These may include spicy sambals, savoury broths, and refreshing concoctions, which give depth of flavour to the dining experience.
