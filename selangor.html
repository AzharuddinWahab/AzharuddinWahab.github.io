Batu Caves, located just outside Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, is a well-known cultural and religious landmark that attracts visitors from all over the world. The location, dominated by a tall limestone hill, contains a succession of caves and cave temples that serve as a significant spiritual and cultural monument.

Inside the caves, a series of caverns and chambers contain Hindu shrines and religious artefacts. The ambiance is mysterious and calm, with a distinct blend of natural wonder and spiritual devotion. The largest cave, known as Cathedral Cave or Temple Cave, has a high ceiling and natural skylights, making it an ideal venue for religious rituals and festivals.

Wau kapal

"Kites : A dance of colors and strings, each one telling a unique story with the wind as its canvas."

Wau Kapal, popularly known as the "ship kite," is a traditional Malaysian kite with a unique and appealing appearance. Wau Kapal, originally from the state of Selangor, has become a symbol of the region's rich cultural heritage and workmanship.

Wau Kapal's shape is distinctive, resembling a ship's hull with an elongated body and curving upper part. These kites, made of bamboo and colourful paper or fabric, are not only aerodynamically engineered for stable flying, but also feature intricate designs and brilliant colours.

Nasi Ambeng

"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication."

Nasi Ambeng Selangor is a delicious culinary jewel from the Malaysian state of Selangor. This classic meal captures the complex tapestry of Malay flavours and communal dining traditions.

Nasi Ambeng Selangor is centred around a big amount of fragrant white rice, which is usually accompanied by a variety of savoury side dishes. These side dishes frequently include:

  • Rendang (a spicy beef stew)
  • Ayam masak merah (chicken in red sauce)
  • Fried shredded coconut
  • Sambal goreng (fried chilli paste with anchovies)
  • A variety of pickles
