MELAKA

Melaka

located on the southwestern coast of Peninsular Malaysia. It is known for its rich historical and cultural heritage and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site

Christ Church

Christ Church built by the Dutch when they took possession of Malacca from the Portuguese, it’s one of Malacca’s most defining structures.

Situated along Jalan Gereja (also known as Church Street) it is an instantly recognizable brick-red building with a huge white cross at the top. Sitting opposite the Stadhuys, Christ Church was built in 1753 to celebrate a century of Dutch occupation.

The interior of the cathedral has 200 year-old handmade pews, decorative fanlights and plaques that honour Dutch soldiers and locals.

Dondang Sayang

"Dondang Sayang" is a traditional Malay song that is very famous in Melaka.The song "Dondang Sayang" is usually performed in a group by two singers, a male (the challenger) and a female (the singer). They engage in a poetic exchange of verses in the song. The verses in "Dondang Sayang" contain various meanings, including expressions of love, joy, appreciation of nature's beauty, and more.

"Dondang Sayang" also holds cultural and historical significance for the people of Melaka. It is often sung at various traditional events such as weddings, festivals, and other cultural occasions.

Asam Pedas Melaka

Asam Pedas Melaka is a traditional Malay dish. It is a spicy and sour tamarind-based stew typically made with fish or seafood, although there are variations that use chicken or beef. Asam Pedas is known for its bold and flavorful taste, combining the sourness of tamarind with the spiciness of chili peppers and a blend of aromatic herbs and spices.

typically served with steamed rice, and its combination of sour and spicy flavors makes it a popular and delicious dish in Malaysian cuisine, especially in the Melaka region.
